Lesson 1 – Strong Passwords & Password Managers

Weak passwords are one of the easiest ways attackers can break into your online shopping or banking accounts. A strong password is long, unique, and hard to guess.

  • Use at least 12 characters.
  • Mix uppercase, lowercase, numbers, and symbols.
  • Do not reuse the same password across banking and shopping sites.
  • Use a password manager to store complex passwords securely.

Scenario: Rohan uses the same password “Rohan123” for his bank and a random shopping site. If the shopping site is hacked, attackers can try the same password on his bank account.

Lesson 2 – Two-Factor Authentication (2FA)

Two-Factor Authentication adds an extra layer of security by requiring something you know (password) and something you have (OTP, authenticator app, security key).

  • Always enable 2FA on your banking and shopping accounts.
  • Use authenticator apps instead of SMS when possible.
  • Never share your OTP with anyone, even if they claim to be from the bank.

Scenario: A fraudster calls saying they’re from your bank and asks you to read the OTP you just received. Sharing it lets them log into your account.

Lesson 3 – Recognizing Fake Shopping Websites

Fake e-commerce websites copy logos and layouts of real brands to trick users into entering card details or login credentials.

  • Check the URL spelling carefully.
  • Look for contact details, return policy, and genuine reviews.
  • Be suspicious of deals that look too good to be true.

Scenario: A site claims “90% off on iPhones – today only!” but the URL is “applestore-discount.net”. This is likely a scam.

Lesson 4 – HTTPS & Secure Connections

HTTPS encrypts data between your browser and the website. It protects your login information and card details from being read by attackers on the network.

  • Always check for https:// and the lock icon before entering sensitive data.
  • A lock icon alone doesn’t guarantee the site is legit, but no lock is a red flag.
  • Avoid entering card details on sites that do not use HTTPS.

Scenario: You open an online banking page and the browser shows “Not secure” in the address bar. You should not log in.

Lesson 5 – Phishing Emails & SMS

Phishing messages pretend to be from real banks or shopping sites to trick you into clicking fake links or sharing personal data.

  • Check the sender’s email address or phone number.
  • Be careful with urgent messages like “Your account will be blocked in 1 hour”.
  • Do not click links in suspicious messages; type the official website URL yourself.

Scenario: You get an email saying “Dear Customer, your bank account is locked. Click here to unlock.” The link goes to a fake login page.

Lesson 6 – OTP & PIN Safety

OTPs (one-time passwords) and PINs are sensitive. If someone gets them, they can complete transactions in your name.

  • Never share OTP or PIN over phone, chat, or SMS.
  • Banks will not ask you to read OTP aloud or send it in WhatsApp.
  • Cover the keypad while entering PIN at ATMs or POS machines.

Scenario: A fake support agent asks you to share OTP to “cancel a transaction”. Sharing it lets them approve the transaction instead.

Lesson 7 – Mobile Banking Safety

Mobile banking apps are convenient but must be used carefully to avoid malware and data theft.

  • Download apps only from official app stores.
  • Keep your phone’s OS and apps updated.
  • Use screen lock and avoid unlocking your phone for strangers.

Scenario: A fake app called “Super Bank Lite” appears in a third-party app store. Installing it can steal your banking login.

Lesson 8 – Public Wi-Fi Risks

Public Wi-Fi networks (cafes, airports) are often unsecured. Attackers can intercept unencrypted traffic on the same network.

  • Avoid online banking or shopping on public Wi-Fi.
  • If you must, use a trusted VPN and ensure HTTPS is enabled.
  • Prefer mobile data (4G/5G) for sensitive transactions.

Scenario: You log into your bank using open airport Wi-Fi. Someone on the network may sniff your data if the connection is not secure.

Lesson 9 – Refund & Support Scams

Scammers pretend to be from customer support (bank or shopping platform) and trick you into sharing details under the excuse of refunds or problem resolution.

  • Do not install remote access apps (like AnyDesk, TeamViewer) on request of unknown agents.
  • Contact support only through official numbers or app chat.
  • Be suspicious if support asks for card details, PIN, or OTP.

Scenario: “Support” calls you to process a refund and asks you to install a remote access app so they can “help”. They can then control your phone.

Lesson 10 – Best Practices Checklist

This lesson summarizes the best practices you should follow to stay safe while shopping and banking online.

  • Use strong, unique passwords + password manager.
  • Enable 2FA on all critical accounts.
  • Check URLs, HTTPS, and avoid suspicious links.
  • Never share OTP, PIN, or full card details.
  • Avoid sensitive transactions on public Wi-Fi.
  • Use only official apps and verified websites.

Scenario: Before entering your card details, you quickly run through your mental checklist: site URL, HTTPS, known brand, and no red flags in the page.
